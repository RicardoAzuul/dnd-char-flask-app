Feats and class features

  • Elven heritage: Immune to magic sleep effects. +2 racial bonus on save vs enchantment spells or effects.
  • Elven training: Martial Weapon Proficiency feats for longsword, rapier, longbow (also composite) and shortbow (also composite)
  • Elven senses: If passing within 5 ft of a secret/concealed door, entitled to Search check as if actively looking
  • Improved Initiative: +4 Initiative.
  • Knack for Magic: You get a +2 bonus on Spellcraft skill checks. In addition, you have an innate talent for magic, granting you the following spell-like abilities as a 1st-level caster: 1/day— detect magic, light, and prestidigitation.
  • Cloaked Casting: +1 to spell DC on spell that targets foe that is denied a Dex bonus to AC: surprised, has not acted yet in combat. +2 on rolls made to overcome the spell resistance of any affected target.
  • Surprise casting: when succesfully using Bluff to feint in combat, target is denied DEX bonus to AC for next melee atk you make, or next spell you cast. Must remain in melee, atk must be made/spell cast on or before your next turn. Feinting = move action (lvl 6). Only useful against humanoids, and opponent gets to add BAB to Sense Motive check
  • Spell Focus (Enchantment) [lvl 3]: +1 to spell DC
  • Spell Focus (Illusion) [lvl 6]: +1 to spell DC
  • Greater Spell Focus (Illusion) [lvl 9]: +1 to spell DC
  • Silent Spell: silent spell is cast with no verbal components. Spells without verbal components are not affected. Spell uses up spell slot one level higher than spell’s actual level.
  • Divination Expertise (Ex): A visionary seeker may add his class level to his caster level when casting divination spells. This bonus stacks with the bonus gained for additional spellcasting levels at every odd-numbered class level. For example, a 10th-level wizard/4th-level visionary seeker would cast divinations as a 16th-level caster (10 for wizard levels, 4 for visionary seeker levels, and 2 for the added spellcasting levels gained from visionary seeker class levels).
  • Spells per Day/Spells Known: At each odd-numbered visionary seeker level, the character gains new spells per day (and spells known, if applicable) as if he had also gained a level in a spellcasting class to which he belonged before adding the prestige class. He does not, however, gain any other benefit a character of that class would have gained (improved undead turning, metamagic or item creation feats, or the like). If the character had more than one spellcasting class before becoming a visionary seeker, the player must decide to which class to add each visionary seeker level for determining spells per day, spells known, and overall caster level.

Languages

Common, Elven, Draconic, Gnoll, Goblin, Orc, Sylvan, Olman
